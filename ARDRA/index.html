Problem Statements

Two real-world challenges that push the boundaries of drone technology. Choose your mission and decode the solution.

01
Environmental Intelligence

Smart Environmental Monitoring with Drones

The Problem

Environmental degradation is accelerating across forests, water bodies, and agricultural regions. Illegal deforestation, water contamination, crop stress, and biodiversity loss often go undetected until significant damage has already occurred. Traditional monitoring relies on manual inspection, satellite imagery, or delayed reporting — methods that are expensive, infrequent, and low-resolution. Drone technology unlocks the potential for real-time, high-resolution monitoring systems that detect threats early and enable rapid intervention.

The Scenario

A vast forest reserve, agricultural region, or watershed demands constant surveillance. Threats range from illegal logging and crop disease to water pollution and land misuse. Ground teams alone cannot cover such enormous areas — aerial monitoring can bridge the gap, but the real challenge lies in transforming raw drone data into actionable environmental intelligence.

Your Mission

Design a drone-enabled environmental monitoring system that detects and tracks sustainability threats across large geographic areas — providing actionable insights to both authorities and communities.

Core Challenges & Deliverables

🛰️ Efficient aerial coverage of large areas
📊 Processing and analyzing environmental data
⚠️ Early detection of ecological threats
📈 Scalable and practical deployment strategies
🤖 AI / Computer Vision for data analysis
🔋 Energy-efficient, economically viable operation

System Constraints

  • Economically viable and scalable
  • Minimal human intervention required
  • Energy-efficient drone operation
  • Minimal disturbance to wildlife and ecosystems
02
Wildlife Protection & Sustainable Design

Anti-Poaching Surveillance & Sustainable Drone Design

The Problem

Illegal wildlife poaching continues to devastate biodiversity across protected forests, particularly in developing conservation landscapes. Traditional patrolling is manpower-intensive, slow, and ineffective over vast, inaccessible terrains. Poachers deploy advanced tools and conduct night operations, making detection far more complex than ever before.

Meanwhile, drones deployed in forest ecosystems face their own challenges — high wind speeds, unpredictable turbulence above tree canopies, and signal interference. Crashes in remote areas leave behind lithium-ion batteries, polymer frames, and electronic boards that pose contamination risks, wildlife hazards, and fire dangers — meaning the very technology designed to protect nature can inadvertently harm it.

Your Dual Mission

Part A — Anti-Poaching Intelligence

Design an AI-powered, scalable drone ecosystem for real-time anti-poaching surveillance that detects suspicious activity day and night, predicts high-risk poaching zones, operates autonomously over large forest areas, and enables rapid ground-team coordination.

Part B — Sustainable Drone Engineering

Develop a wind-resilient and environmentally sustainable drone system that maintains flight stability in turbulent conditions, minimizes crash probability, enables autonomous self-location for retrieval, and uses biodegradable or low-impact materials with safe battery containment.

Key Capabilities Required

🌙 Real-time detection of human activity (day & night)
📍 Predictive analytics for high-risk poaching zones
💨 Wind-resilient flight stability systems
♻️ Biodegradable / recyclable drone materials
🔔 Rapid alerting & ground patrol coordination
📡 Autonomous self-location for crash retrieval

Design Constraints

  • Cost-effective for state forest departments
  • Minimal human intervention for autonomous operations
  • Safe battery containment and eco-friendly disposal
  • Adaptive control systems for turbulent conditions
